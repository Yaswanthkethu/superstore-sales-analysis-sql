Superstore Sales Performance Analysis Using SQL

Project Overview:
This project focuses on analyzing the Superstore retail dataset using SQL to uncover business insights related to sales performance, profitability, customer segments, regional performance, and discount impact. The objective was to transform raw business data into meaningful insights that support data-driven decision-making.

Tools Used:
-MySQL
-SQL

Dataset Information:
* Total Records: 9,994
* Categories: 3
* Sub-Categories: 17
* States: 49
* Regions: 4

SQL Concepts Used:
* SELECT Statements
* WHERE Clause
* Aggregate Functions (SUM, AVG, COUNT)
* GROUP BY
* HAVING
* ORDER BY
* LIMIT
* Subqueries
* Data Validation Queries

Business Questions Answered:
* What is the overall revenue generated by the business?
* Which categories contribute the highest sales and profit?
* Which products generate the highest and lowest profits?
* Which regions and states perform best?
* Which states are generating losses?
* Which customer segment contributes the highest revenue and profit?
* How do discounts impact profitability?
* Which categories and products require business attention?

Key Findings:
* Technology generated the highest sales revenue ($836,154.03).
* Technology generated the highest profit ($145,454.95).
* Consumer Segment generated the highest sales revenue ($1,161,401.34).
* Consumer Segment generated the highest total profit ($134,119.21).
* Home Office Segment achieved the highest profit margin (14.03%).
* West Region generated the highest revenue and profit.
* California and New York were the most profitable states.
* Tables, Bookcases, and Supplies were identified as loss-making sub-categories.
* Furniture received the highest average discount among categories but generated comparatively lower profit.

Business Recommendations:
* Increase focus on high-performing Technology products.
* Optimize discount strategies to improve profitability.
* Improve performance in underperforming regions and states.
* Review loss-making sub-categories and pricing strategies.
* Expand business efforts in profitable markets such as California and New York.
* Continue monitoring key sales and profit metrics for better decision-making.
